Game Jam Experience

There’s a special place in my heart for game jams. They’re chaotic, messy, and brutally short—but somehow, that’s exactly what I love about them. Without deadlines, I tend to overthink ideas and never quite finish them. But in a jam, there’s no time for perfectionism—just raw creativity, caffeine, and a ticking clock pushing you to make something work.

I’ve participated in several major jams like BYOG (Build Your Own Game - India), GMTK Game Jam, and Brackeys Game Jam, each teaching me something new about speed, scope, and sanity. Some projects turned into surprisingly polished games, others... not so much. But every single one helped me sharpen skills that no tutorial could teach—rapid prototyping, creative problem-solving, team communication, and scope control under real pressure.

Game jams taught me to think fast and adapt even faster When something breaks minutes before the deadline (which it always does), you learn to improvise, hack a fix, or design around it entirely. They showed me that done is better than perfect, and sometimes the weirdest, funniest ideas end up being the best ones.

Looking back, the spaghetti code, sleepless nights, and wild last-minute features all feel like battle scars from creative warzones. But that’s the magic of game jams—they remind me why I started making games in the first place. It’s about fun, experimentation, and the thrill of bringing ideas to life under impossible constraints.

Each jam makes me a little better—more confident, more focused, and more comfortable working under pressure. Whether solo or in a team, these experiences shaped how I approach development: iterate fast, fail forward, and always keep that spark of creativity alive.
